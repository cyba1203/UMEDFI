UMEDFI Special Programs

The University of Makati Educational Development Inc. (UMEDFI) continues its mission to enhance academic excellence and research at the University of Makati through specialized programs under the College of Continuing, Advanced, and Professional Studies (CCAPS).

Programs and Leadership

Executive Doctorate in Leadership

Led by Associate Professor Pompeyo C. Adamos II, MA, this program develops leadership innovations and prepares professionals for executive roles.

Special Programs in Allied Health Sciences

Directed by Assistant Professor Felizardo A. Plana, RN, MPA, this includes the Master of Arts in Nursing and the Master of Science in Radiologic Technology.

Special Programs in Business Education

Led by Professor Nemesio T. Barboza, DBA, PhD, including an MBA major in Building and Property Management, MBA in Entrepreneurial Management, and Bachelor of Science in Entrepreneurship.

Special Programs in Technology Management

Professor Loreta E. Catipon, EdD, heads the Bachelor in Industrial Facilities Technology Management and Bachelor in Automotive Technology.

Advanced Programs in Education

Associate Professor Rhene C. Tabajen, PhD, leads the Master of Arts in Innovative Education.

Special Programs in Development Management and Governance

Under Assistant Professor Sonia F. Pimentel, MDMG, DPA, offering a Bachelor of Arts in Political Science major in Local Government Administration.

Future Developments

New training programs under CCAPS are being developed, targeting professionals in the City Government of Makati.

UMEDFI Sets Sights on Changing UMak’s Educational Landscape

The University of Makati Educational Development Inc. (UMEDFI) continues to redirect its previous roots of providing educational assistance to envisioning a partner of excellence in the University of Makati (UMak) through the development of quality academic programs, enhancing the capacity for research and publications, and strengthening a culture of innovation and leadership among its students.

UMEDFI houses six special and executive course programs under the College of Continuing, Advanced and Professional Studies (CCAPS), extending its educational arm to its partner institution in delivering financial sustainability and assistance to part-time college professors.

Undergraduate Programs (Executive)

The Bachelor of Arts in Political Science Major in Local Government Administration, led by Assistant Professor Sonia F. Pimentel, MDMG, DPA, aims to produce graduates proficient in the politico-administrative aspects of managing local government units. This program contributes significantly to UMEDFI’s funding efforts.

Business and Technology Programs

Under the leadership of Professor Loreta E. Catipon, EdD, UMEDFI also funds the Bachelor in Automotive Technology and Bachelor in Industrial Facilities Technology Management. These programs focus on technical skill development and supervisory competencies.

Additionally, Professor Nemesio T. Barboza, DBA, PhD, oversees the Bachelor of Science in Entrepreneurship, MBA major in Building and Property Management, and MBA major in Entrepreneurial Management. These MBA programs integrate interactive learning methodologies for business ventures.

Health Sciences and Education

Under Assistant Professor Felizardo A. Plana, RN, MPA, the Special Programs in Allied Health Sciences include the Master of Arts in Nursing and Master of Science in Radiologic Technology. These programs aim to elevate students' qualifications for leadership roles in healthcare.

Associate Professor Rhene C. Tabajen, PhD, leads the Master of Arts in Innovative Education, designed to modernize pedagogy through technology and critical thinking.

Leadership and Governance

The Executive Doctorate in Leadership, led by Associate Professor Pompeyo C. Adamos II, MA, prepares professionals for leadership innovations, decision-making, and industry-driven solutions through participation in international conferences.

Additionally, the Special Training Programs under Assistant Professor Stephanie Jane Dy-Tiapco focus on professional development for non-UMak personnel, City Hall employees, and the public.
